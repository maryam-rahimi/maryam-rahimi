Maryam Rahimimovassagh

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Central Florida (UCF), advised by Dr. Niloofar Yousefi. My research focuses on advancing bioinformatics through single-cell analysis, multi-omics integration, biomedical imaging, and transcriptomics. I am currently developing AI-driven computational frameworks that combine machine learning, graph-based modeling, and biological data fusion to uncover molecular mechanisms and improve data-driven biomedical discovery. My work aims to enable interpretable, biologically grounded, and robust computational approaches capable of linking cellular-level patterns with system-level behavior. I am also expanding my research into imaging transcriptomics and single-cell multimodal modeling, exploring how gene-expression signatures relate to neural activity, disease progression, and cognitive function. By integrating diverse biological datasets, I aim to create holistic computational models that bridge molecular, cellular, and systems-level understanding in biomedicine.
